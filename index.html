01 / Abstract

The bottleneck is certified coordination.

Renewable-heavy power systems are entering a battery-intensive phase where the core challenge is not only energy generation, but certified coordination of flexibility assets under simultaneous physical, financial, regulatory, and warranty constraints.

ONEDGE ENERGY proposes an open-standard certification layer for BESS operations. Its central object is the Golden Record, which classifies each certified event as compliant, breached, or opaque by binding asset state, dispatch context, telemetry provenance, operating-envelope constraints, state-transition logic, and verification artifacts into an auditable record.

Open standard at the interface. Sovereign proof authority at the core.
02 / System Problem

System dispatch and asset warranties do not speak the same evidence language.

The CEN coordinates Chile's SEN according to system-level objectives such as safety, continuity, economic dispatch, and open access. That role protects the system, but it is not designed to become the warranty arbiter, lender diligence agent, or asset-degradation certifier for every BESS.

A dispatch instruction can be systemically correct and still create asset-level warranty, degradation, or lender-legibility ambiguity.

ONEDGE ENERGY does not merely improve dispatch. It targets the risk premium embedded in operational opacity.
03 / Golden Record

Prove compliance, prove breach, or flag opacity.

The Golden Record creates a repeatable operational evidence object for answering whether a BESS operated in a way that was physically feasible, financially legible, and warranty-defensible.

State 01

Compliant

The asset respected its declared physical, operational, warranty, and contractual envelope.

State 02

Breach

The asset operated outside its declared envelope. This can support OEM attribution and warranty-defense logic.

State 03

Opacity

Required telemetry was missing, corrupt, delayed, inconsistent, non-sequenced, or unverifiable.

ONEDGE ENERGY either proves compliance, proves breach, or flags opacity. It does not convert missing data into false certainty.
04 / Telemetry Boundary

No rip-and-replace. No false positives.

ONEDGE ENERGY does not replace sensor hardware, BMS infrastructure, SCADA systems, meters, inverters, or OEM monitoring systems. The proposed standard requires baselined data provenance from approved asset-originated sources.

The standard requires
  • approved asset-originated telemetry sources;
  • complete, authentic, and sequenced data;
  • operating-envelope parameters;
  • state-transition consistency;
  • verification artifacts tied to declared inputs.
Opacity may include
  • missing or corrupted telemetry;
  • inconsistent BMS / SCADA / meter values;
  • unverified data origin;
  • sensor drift beyond tolerance;
  • mismatch between dispatch instruction and asset-response records.

07 / Market Game

From high deployment with low legibility to high deployment with high legibility.

Without a shared operational standard, each actor rationally protects itself: banks price opacity into WACC, OEMs preserve broad warranty discretion, owners lack standardized proof of prudence, and operators remain exposed after the fact.

ONEDGE ENERGY introduces a shared evidence object. CEN dispatch remains system-sovereign; the BESS response is classified as compliant, breach, or opacity; the owner converts disciplined operation into financial evidence; the OEM gains attribution evidence; and lenders can underwrite operational history.

08 / Validation Path

Chile as reference jurisdiction for global standard formation.

Chile is the proving ground because battery deployment is accelerating, the SEN needs firmness, renewable penetration creates flexibility stress, mining and data centers require reliable energy, and CEN provides a system-level dispatch context.

  1. Define the certification object: dispatch compliance record, warranty-support record, OEM defense record, lender diligence record, operator attribution record, telemetry provenance record, breach record, and opacity-event record.
  2. Reference deployment: TRL-7 shadow-mode validation on real assets under real conditions.
  3. Lender and OEM legibility: convert certified evidence into reusable diligence and warranty language.
  4. Governance file: document that the standard is open at the interface, merit-based, interoperable, non-coercive, and not a foreclosure tool.
  5. Institutional adoption: build referenceability across banks, OEMs, owners, operators, validators, and technical standard institutions.

10 / Claims Discipline

What the whitepaper claims — and what it does not claim.

Claims made
  • ONEDGE ENERGY is a proposed open standard for certified BESS operational evidence.
  • ONEDGE ENERGY is entering TRL-7 validation.
  • ONEDGE ENERGY requires baselined telemetry provenance.
  • ONEDGE ENERGY can classify certified events as compliant, breached, or opaque.

Claims not made

  • It does not prevent degradation.
  • It does not replace CEN dispatch or OEM monitoring systems.
ONEDGE ENERGY reduces the scope and ambiguity of disputes by producing verified operational evidence, breach evidence, or opacity evidence.
